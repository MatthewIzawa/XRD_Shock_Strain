# CIF Sources

These CIF files are bundled with `xrd_profile` for use with the example
scripts and tests. Each file is attributed below with its original
publication and database identifiers. All entries are public-domain
crystallographic data.

## Forsterite (Mg₂SiO₄)

- **File:** `Forsterite.cif`
- **Citation:** Smyth, J. R. & Hazen, R. M. (1973). The crystal
  structures of forsterite and hortonolite at several temperatures
  up to 900 °C. *American Mineralogist* 58, 588-593. (T = 25 °C entry.)
- **Database IDs:** COD 9000319, AMCSD 0000328.
- **Source:** Crystallography Open Database (https://www.crystallography.net/cod/).

## Anorthite (CaAl₂Si₂O₈)

- **File:** `Anorthite.cif`
- **Citation:** Wainwright, J. & Starkey, J. (1971). A refinement of
  the structure of anorthite. *Zeitschrift für Kristallographie* 133,
  75-84.
- **Database IDs:** AMCSD 0010723, COD 1000034.
- **Source:** HOSERLab CIF library (Cloutis), originally from
  Wainwright & Starkey (1971). The same lattice parameters are used
  inline in the existing `synchrotron_low_shock.py` example.

## Pigeonite (Ca₀.₇₂Mg₃.₁₂Fe₄.₁₆Si₈O₂₄)

- **File:** `Pigeonite.cif`
- **Citation:** Morimoto, N. & Güven, N. (1970). Refinement of the
  crystal structure of pigeonite. *American Mineralogist* 55,
  1195-1209.
- **Database ID:** AMCSD 0000209.
- **Source:** HOSERLab CIF library (Cloutis), originally from
  Morimoto & Güven (1970).

## Quartz (SiO₂, low form)

- **File:** `Quartz.cif`
- **Citation:** Levien, L., Prewitt, C. T. & Weidner, D. J. (1980).
  Structure and elastic properties of quartz at pressure.
  *American Mineralogist* 65, 920-930. (P = 1 atm entry.)
- **Database IDs:** COD 9000775, AMCSD 0000789.
- **Source:** Crystallography Open Database.

## Hematite (Fe₂O₃)

- **File:** `Hematite.cif`
- **Citation:** Blake, R. L., Hessevick, R. E., Zoltai, T. &
  Finger, L. W. (1966). Refinement of the hematite structure.
  *American Mineralogist* 51, 123-129.
- **Database ID:** AMCSD 0000143.
- **Source:** HOSERLab CIF library (Cloutis), originally from
  Blake et al. (1966).
